What is impressionism?

Impressionism is an artistic movement first established in the late 19th which was not in any other country more sensational than France. Being called impressionism was that the artists were not trying to make the paintings realistic, but they tried to capture the 'impression' of the scenario they see 'on the spot'. Unlike the academic rules that the artists would sketch roughly from what they saw then finished works in the studios. This art style was invented by Parisian artists, and one of them was Claude Monet.
